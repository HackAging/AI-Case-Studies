# GPT-5 Wet Lab Protocol Optimization

## Description

A December 2025 research demonstration project where GPT-5 optimized a standard molecular cloning protocol, achieving a 79x efficiency improvement. This represents the first demonstration of large language models directly improving wet lab experimental efficiency at this scale.

## Lessons Learned

### Achievements

- First demonstration of LLMs directly improving wet lab experimental efficiency at scale (79x improvement)
- Successful integration of AI reasoning with laboratory experimentation
- Demonstrated AI's ability to propose novel mechanisms (RecA+gp32 combination)
- Validated approach through independent replicate experiments
- Established framework for AI-assisted protocol optimization

### Implications

- AI can work alongside biologists to accelerate research and reduce costs
- Systematic protocol optimization at scale is now feasible
- High-throughput protocol optimization can enable new types of experiments
- AI-native labs become practical for longevity research
- Potential to compress research timelines by 2-3x if applied broadly

### Challenges

- Requires careful biosecurity considerations
- Need for controlled experimental systems
- Validation through independent experiments is essential
- Results may be specific to the experimental system used

### Impact on Field

This project demonstrates that AI can significantly accelerate biological research by optimizing laboratory protocols. The 79x efficiency improvement shows the potential for AI to work alongside scientists, reducing time and costs. If applied broadly across aging research, this could compress research timelines by 2-3x.
